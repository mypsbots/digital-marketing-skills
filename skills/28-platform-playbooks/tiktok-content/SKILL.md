## Overview

Defines a TikTok strategy: native, entertaining/educational short-form video; strong 1–3s hooks;
authentic use of trends/sounds; repeatable series; posting cadence; and community engagement — with
a native, non-salesy tone.

## Business purpose

TikTok offers outsized organic reach to non-followers via the For You feed; native, hook-driven
content wins discovery.

## When to use / when not to use

- **Use** to plan an organic TikTok presence.
- **Do not use** overly polished ads-as-content, misused sounds, or undisclosed promotions.

## Inputs

### Required
- Brand goal, audience.

### Optional
- Existing performance, competitors, creators.

## Outputs

- A **TikTok strategy** (goal → content angles).
- **Content formats and hooks** (series, trends, educational, UGC).
- A **cadence and trend plan** (frequency, sound/trend monitoring).

## Methodology

1. Define the audience and a native, authentic brand voice.
2. Build repeatable content formats/series around value and entertainment.
3. Engineer strong 1–3s hooks for every video.
4. Participate in relevant trends/sounds authentically and quickly.
5. Set a high, sustainable cadence; engage comments; iterate on hook/retention data.

## Decision rules

- Native and authentic over polished ads.
- Hook in the first seconds; sound-on culture but caption too.
- Use only licensed/available sounds; disclose ads.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Native formats/series + strong hooks.
- [ ] Trend approach + cadence.
- [ ] Community engagement plan.

## Compliance checklist

- [ ] Disclose partnerships; sound/music rights.
- [ ] Community guidelines; no misleading claims.

## Failure conditions and escalation

- Missing goal/audience → `insufficient_data`.

## Examples

- **SaaS:** relatable "day in the workflow" + tips.
- **E-commerce:** demos, UGC, trend-jacking products.
- **Real estate:** quick tours + market tips.
- **Retail:** trends, hauls, behind-the-scenes.

## KPIs and metrics

Reach/FYP views, hook rate, completion, shares/saves, follower growth.
