## Overview

Defines an X/Twitter strategy: concise value posts, well-structured threads, timely replies and
conversation, and community engagement — with a native, fast, authentic voice suited to real-time
discourse.

## Business purpose

X rewards timely, conversational authority and can drive outsized reach via replies and threads for
the right audiences (tech, media, B2B, news-adjacent).

## When to use / when not to use

- **Use** to plan an X presence, threads and engagement.
- **Do not use** for outrage-bait, misinformation, or undisclosed promotions.

## Inputs

### Required
- Goal, audience.

### Optional
- Voice, competitors, topics.

## Outputs

- An **X strategy** (goal → content angles).
- **Content formats** (single posts, threads, replies, quote posts).
- A **cadence and engagement plan** (posting + active reply strategy).

## Methodology

1. Define voice and topics of authority; pick lanes.
2. Mix formats: punchy value posts, threads for depth, replies for reach.
3. Write strong opening lines; make threads scannable with a clear payoff.
4. Engage in real time on relevant conversations (reply-first growth).
5. Set cadence; monitor what resonates and double down.

## Decision rules

- Conversation/replies drive reach as much as posting.
- Concise, native, timely; threads must deliver the promised payoff.
- No misinformation/outrage-bait; disclose ads.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Voice + topic lanes.
- [ ] Format mix incl. threads.
- [ ] Reply/engagement routine.

## Compliance checklist

- [ ] Disclose partnerships; accurate claims.
- [ ] Rights to media; platform rules.

## Failure conditions and escalation

- Missing goal/audience → `insufficient_data`.

## Examples

- **SaaS:** build-in-public threads + tips.
- **E-commerce:** launches + customer replies.
- **Real estate:** market takes + local news.
- **Retail:** timely offers + trend commentary.

## KPIs and metrics

Impressions, engagement rate, profile visits, follower growth, link clicks.
