## Overview

Builds an authentic brand narrative and a clear point of view that connect to what the audience
dreams, fears and believes. Stories aligned to a group's worldview ("people like us do things
like this") are what make ideas spread — far more than feature lists.

## Business purpose

A resonant, consistent story differentiates the brand, earns attention and trust, and gives the
audience something to belong to and repeat — fuelling organic word of mouth.

## When to use / when not to use

- **Use** to define a brand narrative, articulate a point of view, or brief story-led content.
- **Do not use** to fabricate a founder/origin story or manufacture values you don't hold —
  inauthentic narrative erodes trust fast.

## Inputs

### Required
- Brand purpose/values, target-audience worldview, offering.

### Optional
- Voice of customer, founder story, proof/evidence.

## Outputs

- A **brand narrative** (core story and themes).
- A **point-of-view statement** (what you stand for and against).
- **Story arcs** for reuse across channels.
- A **worldview alignment map** (audience beliefs ↔ brand story).

## Methodology

1. Clarify what the brand stands for (purpose, values, the change it seeks).
2. Map the target audience's worldview: dreams, fears, identity, "people like us".
3. Find the honest intersection of brand truth and audience worldview.
4. Craft the core narrative (who it's for, the tension, the transformation) — the hero is the customer.
5. Articulate a clear point of view — stand for something specific and meaningful.
6. Derive reusable story arcs and proof; keep it consistent across touchpoints.
7. Test resonance with real audience feedback and refine.

## Decision rules

- Make the customer the hero; the brand is the guide.
- Align to the audience's worldview rather than trying to change it.
- Keep it authentic and evidenced; never invent stories or values.

## Quality checklist

- [ ] Narrative ties brand truth to audience worldview.
- [ ] Point of view is specific and meaningful.
- [ ] Stories are authentic and reusable across channels.

## Compliance checklist

- [ ] Claims and testimonials within stories substantiated.
- [ ] No fabricated origin/impact stories; endorsements disclosed.

## Failure conditions and escalation

- Missing purpose/worldview/offering → `insufficient_data`.
- Sensitive social/identity claims → brand and legal review.

## Examples

- **SaaS:** a point of view on how work should change, customer as hero of the transformation.
- **E-commerce:** a values-led origin story aligned to a subculture's identity.
- **Real estate:** a local, community-rooted narrative of trust and belonging.
- **Retail:** a brand stance (craft, sustainability) that customers want to belong to.

## KPIs and metrics

Brand recall/affinity, message resonance, share/word-of-mouth of story content, and assisted
conversions from brand content.
